=== Sustainability Cell IIT Bombay - Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ===

This FAQ contains direct answers to the most commonly asked questions about the Sustainability Cell at IIT Bombay.

--- General Questions ---

Q: What is the Sustainability Cell?
A: The Sustainability Cell at IIT Bombay is a student-driven initiative dedicated to promoting sustainable practices and environmental consciousness within the campus and beyond. We work towards creating a more sustainable future through various projects, events, and awareness campaigns.

Q: What is the mission of Sustainability Cell?
A: Our mission is to integrate sustainability into every aspect of campus life, from waste management to energy conservation, while fostering a culture of environmental responsibility among students, faculty, and staff.

Q: What are the core focus areas of Sustainability Cell?
A: The three core focus areas are: 1) Innovation - Developing and implementing innovative solutions for sustainability challenges, 2) Education - Spreading awareness and knowledge about sustainable practices, and 3) Community - Building a strong community of sustainability advocates and practitioners.

Q: How many members does Sustainability Cell have?
A: The Sustainability Cell has 20+ active members, has completed 10+ projects, organized 15+ events, and impacted 1000+ students.

--- Events Questions ---

Q: What events does Sustainability Cell organize?
A: We organize various events including competitions (Sustainium Case Competition, SustAInify, Airathon Mumbai), talks (Youth in Climate Action, Environmental Defence Fund sessions), workshops (ESG Workshop), awareness campaigns (#BreatheBetterMumbai), and orientation events.

Q: What is Sustainium Case Competition?
A: Sustainium is a nationwide case competition organized in collaboration with Tarutium Global Consulting, focusing on innovative waste management solutions. It offers cash prizes and pre-placement interview (PPI) opportunities with 500+ registrations.

Q: What is SustAInify?
A: SustAInify is a competition to address real-world sustainability issues using machine learning. It focuses on data-driven climate solutions with Rs. 30,000 in cash prizes.

Q: What is Airathon Mumbai?
A: Airathon Mumbai was a pan-India competition to enhance Mumbai's air quality, held in collaboration with the Institute Technical Council. It had two tracks (Technical and Campaign) with Rs. 60,000 in prizes and 150+ submissions. It was Asia's largest and world's second-largest air quality competition.

Q: What is the Green Cup initiative?
A: Green Cup is an inter-hostel sustainability competition launched in collaboration with HA Council. It introduces a scoring system for hostels based on their sustainability practices. The launch event had 100+ attendees from all hostels.

--- Campus Projects Questions ---

Q: What campus projects has Sustainability Cell implemented?
A: Major implementations include: EV Buggy Stops optimization, AC Optimization in LHC, Waste Data Display Boards in Mess, Tree Plantation drives, Swacchta Pakhwada support, Bin Segregation Project, Carbon Footprinting assessment, and the Green Cup initiative.

Q: What is the Bin Segregation Project?
A: The Bin Segregation Project is a waste bin segregation initiative at IIT Bombay in association with Hostel Affairs Council. It aims for efficient waste disposal, recycling, and reducing the campus carbon footprint.

Q: What is the Carbon Footprinting project?
A: The Carbon Footprinting project is a collaboration with Professor Vikram Vishal (Earth Science) and Professor Arnab Dutta (Chemistry) to assess IIT Bombay's carbon footprint. The emission breakdown shows: 95% from Scope 2, 4.4% from Scope 1, and 0.6% from Scope 3.

Q: What are the Waste Data Display Boards?
A: Waste Data Display Boards are integrated into every hostel to track and reduce food wastage. They show a transparent view of daily food wastage and engage residents in reducing their environmental footprint.

--- Sustainability Initiatives Questions ---

Q: What is the #BreatheBetterMumbai campaign?
A: #BreatheBetterMumbai is an air pollution awareness campaign run during the IITB Half Marathon. It partners with certified recycling organizations for plastic bottle recycling and features winning projects from Airathon Mumbai.

Q: What sustainability workshops does Sustainability Cell conduct?
A: We conduct workshops like the ESG Workshop on 'People, Planet, and Profits' conducted by Captain Tapas Majumdar, focusing on Environmental, Social, and Governance aspects in the corporate sector. The workshop had 80+ participants.

Q: Does Sustainability Cell collaborate with other organizations?
A: Yes, we collaborate with various organizations including Team Shunya, Hostel Affairs Council, Institute Technical Council, Tarutium Global Consulting, Fitizen India, Environmental Defence Fund, and faculty members like Professor Vikram Vishal and Professor Arnab Dutta.
